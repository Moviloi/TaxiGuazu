The Brazilian side
seen the right way

Migration assistance

We guide you at every step of the crossing: Argentine customs, Tancredo Neves bridge, Brazilian customs. With your documentation in order, the crossing usually takes between 15 and 30 minutes.

Brazilian side falls

The most impressive panoramic view of the Falls. Iguaçu National Park has a walkway circuit to Devil's Throat. Access via the park's internal bus.

Parque das Aves — optional

300 meters from the Brazilian park entrance. Over 1,500 birds from the Atlantic Forest. Can be combined on the same day. Let us know if you want to include it.

Itaipu Dam — optional

The Brazil–Paraguay binational dam, one of the largest in the world. Guided tours available during the day. Can be combined with the falls in a full day.

Bilingual driver

We speak Spanish and Portuguese. At the border crossing we guide you in both languages. We also answer inquiries in English via WhatsApp.

Reais and Dollars

In Brazil you need Reais for tickets. We guide you on how much to bring and where to exchange. We also accept Pesos and Dollars for the transfer.

What you need
to cross to Brazil

  1. 1
    Documentation

    Valid passport for foreigners. For Argentines, national ID is enough to enter Brazil.

  2. 2
    Migration declaration

    At the crossing they give you a form. We help you fill it out. It's quick.

  3. 3
    Money in Reais

    Park tickets are paid in Reais. You can exchange at the crossing or in Foz.

  4. 4
    Comfortable clothing

    The Brazilian park circuit is long. Bring comfortable shoes and water.
